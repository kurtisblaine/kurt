 F U E L I N G   O P S 


Aided in the responsibilities of an Air Support Squadron: tested the fuel by water, sediment, and visual inspections, created isolated, discrete forward arming and refueling points (FARPs) for expedited hot-fueling operations, delivered cold fuel to aircraft stationed on flight lines, and de-fueled mechanically inoperable aircraft for maintenance in bay areas; responsibilities were done in a militaristic, professional manner in high regards to the establishment and infrastructure of the United States Military.

Aided in air refueling/defueling operations in Cold Lake, Alberta, Canada; worked with NATO members from all over the globe and familiarized ourselves with foreign aircraft, fuel distributions, and culture. We learned the fueling systems of these countries, the different types of aircraft their pilots fly, and how to refuel them.

Aided in ground refueling/defueling operations in Old Harbour, AL, specifically, heavy equipment, HMMVs, and generators. Worked in rotation between heavy equipment operator and fueling specialist to build a flight line for the locals in the city of Old Harbour. After a collective effort by hundreds of Marines over a six year duration, the flight line was built and the AC 130 landed. You can read about this event here

 L E A D E R S H I P 

I am currently a lance corporal and I have been in the Marine Corps for four years as a reservist and I couldn't be more proud of where I have been and what I have accomplished. From the moment I arrived in San Deigo, I was blessed with leaders that taught and molded me into the person I am today. I have learned so much from them and I do not take any of it for granted. Also, being a young service member in the military, we are considered future leaders. A common trait that we learned quickly was leadership; whether it is being taught in class, in real-life situations, in a hard way, or in seminars. Leadership is, in my eyes, one of the most important and challenging traits to master and I hope to be a great leader one day.
