 D   R   U   M   S 

My entire family is musically gifted; to no avail, I would have at least some musical savvy. I have been playing the drums for 6 years--either an electronic set or an acoustic set; living constraints, unfortunately, limit my habitual playing. Growing up, my parents bought us 'Rock Band'--you know the game where you simulate an actual rock band with plastic instruments--and instantly, was hooked. After that, I saved up my money to nab an actual drum set.

My brothers also play the drums and guitar, my sister likes to think she can sing, and, back in the day, my father played guitar in a band; you can search 'The Waldner Messengers' on google or if you like Country/Soul, you can listen to them below.

  B   A   N   G   O  

With the living constraints mentioned earlier--having neighbors--I decided learning a new instrument was a good idea; hence, welcome the banjo! I bought my first banjo about a year ago with the initial thought, 'if I can shred it on the banjo, then I can shred it on the guitar.' So, I have been learning--with a little help from ebooks, YouTube, and finger picks--the forward, reverse, alternative, and lick rolls associated with the songs I know. With more time, I hope to do these roles as fast as humanity possible.
